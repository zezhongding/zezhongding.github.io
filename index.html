    Research Interests

    Graph Clustering Algorithms. Graphs are used in a wide variety of applications, from biology to social media to network analysis. Most of these graphs have a similar structure: nodes form groups through tight and complex connections, and nodes within a group are sparsely connected to other nodes. In the study of such graphs, one of the most fundamental questions is how to identify these dense clusters of nodes. This problem is often referred to as graph clustering or community detection. Graph clustering is a core problem in graph analysis. Most existing methods focus on finding partitions of graph nodes. A major challenge facing graph clustering algorithms is their ability to handle large-scale graphs that are common in a large number of domains.

    Graph Neural Network. In recent years, there has been increasing interest in the extension of deep learning methods to graphs. Driven by the success of many factors, researchers defined and designed Neural network structures for Graph data processing by referring to the ideas of convolutional Networks, cyclic Networks and deep autoencoders. Thus, a new research hotspot -- Graph Neural Networks (GNN) emerged.
